# 📅 April 18 – Cyber Security Learning Log

## ⚠️ Risks of Sharing Information on Social Media
Sharing information on social media can expose users and organizations to various security risks.

### 🔹 Common Risks:
- 🕵️ **Information Leakage** – Personal or sensitive data becomes public
- 🎭 **Identity Theft** – Attackers misuse personal details
- 📍 **Location Tracking** – Revealing real-time location
- 🔓 **Account Hacking** – Weak privacy settings can be exploited
- 📉 **Reputation Damage** – Inappropriate or leaked content

---

## 🕵️ How Hackers Gather Intelligence Online
Hackers use various techniques to collect information about individuals or organizations.

### 🔹 Methods:
- 🔍 **Social Media Profiling** – Analyzing posts, photos, and connections
- 🌐 **Open Source Intelligence (OSINT)** – Collecting publicly available data
- 📧 **Phishing Campaigns** – Tricking users into revealing information
- 🧩 **Data Aggregation** – Combining data from multiple sources

### 🔹 Purpose:
- Plan targeted attacks
- Steal identities or credentials
- Exploit system vulnerabilities

---

## 📧 Identifying Phishing Emails
Phishing emails attempt to trick users into revealing sensitive information.

### 🔹 Signs of Phishing:
- 🚫 Unknown or suspicious sender
- 🔗 Fake or mismatched URLs
- ⚠️ Urgent or threatening language
- 📎 Unexpected attachments
- ❌ Poor grammar or spelling errors

### 🔹 Safety Tips:
- Verify sender before clicking links
- Avoid downloading unknown attachments
- Use email filtering and security tools
- Report suspicious emails immediately

---

## 📶 Recognizing Authentic vs Fake Wi-Fi Networks

### 🔹 Authentic Wi-Fi Networks:
- 🔐 Password-protected (WPA2/WPA3)
- 📛 Official network name (SSID)
- 🏢 Provided by trusted sources (home, office, café)
- 🔒 Requires secure login

---

### 🔹 Fake Wi-Fi Networks (Rogue/ Evil Twin):
- 📡 Open or weakly secured networks
- 📛 Names similar to legitimate networks
- 🎭 Created by attackers to lure users
- 🕵️ Used to intercept data (MITM attacks)

---

### 🔹 Safety Tips:
- Verify network name with provider
- Avoid connecting to open/public Wi-Fi
- Use VPN for secure browsing
- Turn off auto-connect feature

---

## ✅ Summary
- Social media sharing can lead to privacy and security risks
- Hackers gather intelligence using OSINT and profiling techniques
- Phishing emails can be identified by suspicious signs
- Fake Wi-Fi networks are used to steal data; always verify before connecting
