## 📡 Different Types of Wireless Networks

Wireless networks can be classified based on their coverage area and usage. The most common types are **LAN, WAN, and PAN**.

---

## 🏠 LAN (Local Area Network)
A **Local Area Network (LAN)** connects devices within a small geographical area such as a home, school, or office.

### 🔹 Characteristics:
- Limited coverage (few meters to a building)
- High data transfer speed
- Privately owned and managed
- Low latency

### 🔹 Use Cases:
- Home Wi-Fi networks
- Office networks
- Computer labs in schools/colleges

---

## 🌍 WAN (Wide Area Network)
A **Wide Area Network (WAN)** connects devices over a large geographical area, such as cities, countries, or even globally.

### 🔹 Characteristics:
- Covers large distances
- Lower speed compared to LAN
- Uses public and private communication lines
- Higher latency

### 🔹 Use Cases:
- The Internet (largest WAN)
- Banking networks
- Corporate branch connectivity across cities

---

## 📱 PAN (Personal Area Network)
A **Personal Area Network (PAN)** connects devices within a very short range, typically around an individual person.

### 🔹 Characteristics:
- Very small coverage (a few meters)
- Low power consumption
- Simple setup
- Often uses Bluetooth or infrared

### 🔹 Use Cases:
- Connecting smartphone to earbuds (Bluetooth)
- Smartwatches and fitness bands
- Wireless keyboards and mice

---

## ⚖️ Comparison of Network Types

| Feature        | LAN 🏠                | WAN 🌍                  | PAN 📱                |
|----------------|---------------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| Coverage Area  | Small               | Very Large             | Very Small           |
| Speed          | High                | Medium to Low          | Low                  |
| Ownership      | Private             | Public/Private         | Personal             |
| Examples       | Home/Office Wi-Fi   | Internet               | Bluetooth devices    |

---

## ✅ Summary
- **LAN** is used for small, high-speed local connections
- **WAN** connects networks over large distances (like the internet)
- **PAN** is used for short-range personal device communication
- Each network type serves different purposes based on coverage and usage
